2) Information hierarchy — Are we front-loading the right things?
- Strengths: Clear hero with strong claim + Telegram CTA. Good.
- Issues: Social proof and credibility come after hero, but pricing is far down (5th main section). Reader hits pricing too late.
- Fix: Put pricing above the fold on desktop via a “from €19/mo” price anchor in hero and a compact pricing section by screen ~2. Add a mini feature list right under hero CTA to validate value quickly.

3) Section-by-section verdict
- Hero: KEEP, but simplify subhead and add a price anchor (“From €19/mo”). Keep one social proof strip (OpenClaw + stars) directly under CTA; drop provider grid or compress to a single line.
- Stats: CUT or MERGE into a single credibility line under hero (“Built on OpenClaw • 215k+ stars”). Full stat bar isn’t necessary.
- Capabilities (What Your AI Can Do): SIMPLIFY. Reduce to 4 punchy bullets: Email/Calendar, Actions (book, reply, fill forms), Memory, Proactive. Drop “Your AI Team,” “Browse & Monitor” (fold into Actions), and keep copy to one line each.
- Comparison (Not Just Another Chatbot): CUT. The table is redundant and slows the scroll.
- How it Works: SIMPLIFY to 3 steps, 1 short line each. Keep.
- Why ClawHatch (features list): MERGE into Capabilities (or a short “Why us” band with 3 value props: Managed, Private, Lives in Telegram). Do not keep a second long grid.
- Pricing: KEEP and MOVE UP (immediately after short capabilities/why band). Trim bullets to the unique value that’s not already stated above. Remove strike-through pricing or keep only on launch if temporary.
- FAQ: SIMPLIFY to 4–5 Qs tops: What is it? Do I need API keys? Is data private? Usage limits? Cancel? Remove “How is this different from ChatGPT?” (covered already) and “WhatsApp?” move to hero microcopy.
- Footer: KEEP minimal.

4) Word count and scroll depth
- Current page is verbose with multiple grids and repeated phrasing. Estimated ~900–1,200 words of body copy.
- Target: ~450–650 words total body copy.
- Ideal scroll depth before pricing: Pricing visible by screen 1.5–2 on desktop, screen 2–3 on mobile. Include “From €19/mo” in hero to satisfy price curiosity immediately.

5) Tone consistency
- Overall tone: crisp, benefits-forward, slightly hypey but acceptable for consumer SaaS. Visuals feel premium.

Some bullets slip into technical (“50+ skills & integrations”) without tying to outcomes. Keep the tone punchy, outcome-first, reduce internal jargon (“group AI,” “team of AIs”).
